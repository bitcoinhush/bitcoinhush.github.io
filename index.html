How Do I Get my Airdrop?

In order to receive Bitcoin Hush during the airdrop, you must hold your coins in a wallet address where you are the owner of the private keys. (Do NOT hold your coins on an exchange!) If you are holding HUSH, be sure you are holding your coins in TRANSPARENT addresses (taddrs), and NOT SHIELDED addresses (zaddrs). If you own HUSH on a zaddr your total balance is shielded from public view, and there can be no way to confirm how many coins you have. Therefore, you cannot be compensated BTCH when the airdrop occurs. You must transfer any coins from any zaddrs to taddrs to receive your BTCH. If you wish to keep the shielding privacy granted by zaddrs through ZK-Snarks, you may do so at the cost of not getting BTCH when the airdrop occurs.

The Bitcoin Hush Airdrop will occur at a block height closest to (but not later than) 12pm NOON UTC on Feb 1st 2018

For each chain will be chosen at the “UTXO migration set”, make sure to HODL your coins until then. Unconfirmed transactions in flight WILL NOT BE COUNTED. The mempool will be ignored!

  • For each 1 HUSH, you will get 1 BTCH.
  • For each 1 SUPERNET/Unity, you will get 1 BTCH.
  • For each 1 DEX, you will get 1 BTCH.
  • For each 1 BTC, you will get 1 BTCH.

For any other cryptocurrency or fork, you get nothing. (e.g. Owning Bitcoin Cash or Dogecoin will not get you any BTCH).

Will everything be done and launch at the above specified time?

No, the full network is expected to launch some time in February, since there needs to be time to decide on a final value for each taddr, and then make many many transactions on the BTCH chain to fill the appropriate taddrs with value on the BTCH chain.

What is Bitcoin Hush?

BTCH is a KMD asset chain with imported UTXO dataset. It is NOT a chain split nor code fork of HUSH, nor ZEC, nor BTC.

BTCH takes the value from four different blockchains (HUSH,SUPERNET,DEX,BTC), and then dumps it onto a host chain Komodo (KMD). It does not fork from KMD, nor does it fork from BTC or HUSH. It is technically NOT A FORK, but a merged airdrop.

BTCH is actually a Komodo asset chain with all the money from four other different blockchains, mixed into one big bag. Also, BTCH is protected by the full hashpower of Bitcoin because Komodo has delayed Proof-Of-Work (dPOW) which syncs the KMD blockchain to the BTC blockchain occasionally. This means that if you had malicious intent and wanted to compromise the BTCH chain, you must first have enough hash power to mess with BTC itself to then subsequently also be able to mess with KMD OR a KMD asset chain.

What is the Hushlist Protocol?

HushList is a protocol for mailing lists using the encrypted memo field of the Zcash protocol. It supports anonymous and pseudonymous senders, anonymous receivers and Hushlist creators, as well as public and private lists. In addition to the above properties, HushList provides users with censorship-resistant storage and retrieval, since every Hush full node will have an encrypted copy of every HushList memo. Furthermore, sending and receiving via one or more blockchains is a serious deviation from traditional serverclient design which easily allows a Man-In-The-Middle Attack and Deep Packet Inspection (DPI).

You can read the full pre-release whitepaper for the Hushlist Protocol here.

What will happen to Hush?

Hush is the mother chain to BTCH, and has it’s own network, it’s not going anywhere. BTCH is a new coin to try out things that are just simply impossible with one blockchain that already exists.

HUSH still has a low circulating supply (less than 15%) and will have on-chain public HushLists before any other coin. HushNG (Hush's all-in-one high speed messaging, transaction, and asset platform) is also about to be released.

Additionally, Hush has very low circulating supply (less than 20% in circulation) and is minable, while BTCH will not be minable and the entire supply will already be in circulation.
